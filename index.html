Khalid ibn al-Walid

One of the Three greatest warriors of all time.

Born in 6th century CE in Mecca, titled "Sword Of God", Khālid ibn al-Walīd ibn al-Mughīra al-Makhzūmī was an Arab military commander who led the muslim arabs to victory in over a hundred battles against the numerically superior armies of Rome, Byzantium and the Persians, not losing even once.

Here are some of the feats he accomplished during his life:

  • Unified Arabia for the first time in history in one year after starting his campaign in the ridda wars.
  • Fought and won along with his cousin against 12 persian men and their general after they tried to ambush him.
  • Never lost a single battle , only not winning his very first battle which he got command of after his army had lost, and even then he made an impressive tactical retreat to minimize casualties.
  • After he is ordered to capture damascus, he marched through a vast water less desert between Syria and Iraq in an unprecedented logistical feat.
  • Captured Iraq in one year.
  • Defeated 120 thousand romans with 40 thousand muslim arabs in the battle of Yarmouk.
